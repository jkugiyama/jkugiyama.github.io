## What are Design Patterns?

Design patterns are the general solutions to problems that software engineers commonly run into. From creation, to structure, or behavior, design patterns can be the first step in resolving an issue. They aren’t specific to problems, but more so an outline that can be customized in order to fit the needs of a problem. Design patterns are sometimes not even a piece of code itself, but more so an approach to a problem. 

## Design Patterns I Have Used Before

A design pattern I’ve implemented before is the factory method, which is the idea of having an interface for creating an object in a superclass, but includes subclasses to alter the objects. In my project, Fishing Frenzy Bash, my group and I were able to use this concept for creating fish objects. We had a superclass that generalized the basics of a fish, and then had subclasses to make specific notions of a fish family.
